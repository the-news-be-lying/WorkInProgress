## 1. The Theological Foundation: Dispensationalism and Christian Zionism (1830s–1909)

**John Nelson Darby and the Pre-Tribulation Rapture**

In the 1830s, Anglo-Irish theologian John Nelson Darby (1800–1882) of the Plymouth Brethren movement developed the framework now known as dispensationalism — a reading of scripture dividing human history into distinct "dispensations" governed by God, culminating in a literal millennial kingdom on Earth. Darby introduced the doctrine of the pre-tribulation rapture: the belief that Christians would be physically removed from Earth before a seven-year period of tribulation, after which Jesus would return to reign from Jerusalem. Central to his system was a sharp distinction between the Church and ethnic Israel — with the latter holding a distinct, literal, and prophetic destiny involving the physical land of Palestine. [[Wikipedia, Dispensationalism]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dispensationalism) [[Bible Rebel, Sep 5, 2025]](https://biblerebel.com)

**Christian Restorationism in Britain**

- **1840:** Lord Palmerston (British Foreign Secretary) advocated for Jewish resettlement in Palestine on strategic and theological grounds
- **1841:** A "Memorandum to Protestant Monarchs of Europe for the Restoration of the Jews to Palestine" circulated among evangelical circles
- **1840s–1880s:** Lord Shaftesbury (7th Earl), a deeply evangelical politician, lobbied for Jewish restoration, viewing it as a prophetic imperative — establishing the template that would eventually influence the Balfour Declaration

[[Wikipedia, Christian Zionism]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Zionism) [[Britain Palestine Project, Stephen Sizer]](https://britainpalestineproject.org) [[CIAO/Columbia University]](https://ciaotest.cc.columbia.edu)

**William Blackstone and American Christian Zionism**

In 1887, American evangelical William Eugene Blackstone (1841–1935) published *Jesus is Coming*, eventually translated into 36 languages by 1927. His 1891 "Blackstone Memorial" — a petition to President Benjamin Harrison signed by 413 prominent Americans including John D. Rockefeller and the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court — called for a Jewish state in Palestine. Blackstone's framework was explicitly dispensationalist: Jewish return to Palestine was a prerequisite for Christ's Second Coming. Some Israeli historians called him the "Father of Zionism." [[Liberty University/Pre-Trib Research Center]](https://digitalcommons.liberty.edu) [[WRMEA, 2015]](https://www.wrmea.org)

## 2. Scofield's Bible and the Zionization of American Evangelicalism (1909–1948)

**The Scofield Reference Bible**

In 1909, Cyrus Ingerson Scofield (1843–1921) published the *Scofield Reference Bible* — an annotated King James Bible with dispensationalist commentary embedded directly into the text. Published by Oxford University Press and revised in 1917, it became one of the most influential religious texts in American history. Scofield's footnotes identified Russia as "Gog" and "Magog" from Ezekiel 38–39, and framed the Jewish return to Palestine as a prophetic necessity preceding Christ's return. One researcher concluded that the Scofield Bible effectively "made Zionists of America's evangelical Christians." [[Wikipedia, Scofield Reference Bible]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scofield_Reference_Bible) [[WRMEA, 2015]](https://www.wrmea.org) [[Catalyst Ministries]](https://catalystministries.org)

**Oxford University Press and the Exclusive Brethren Network**

Scofield's connection to Oxford University Press was facilitated through the Exclusive Brethren network — the same movement Darby had founded. After Scofield's death in 1921, Oxford University Press continued revising his notes, increasingly incorporating pro-Israel theological commentary following Israel's 1947–1948 founding. Critics noted that this made the Scofield Bible a vehicle for embedding Christian Zionist theology directly into the most widely distributed annotated Bible in American Protestant history. [[WRMEA, 2015]](https://www.wrmea.org) [[Catalyst Ministries]](https://catalystministries.org)

**The Balfour Declaration and Evangelical Influence**

- **November 2, 1917:** British Foreign Secretary Arthur Balfour issued the Balfour Declaration — a letter to Lord Walter Rothschild stating that "His Majesty's Government view with favour the establishment in Palestine of a national home for the Jewish people"
- Balfour was raised in an evangelical household and regarded history as "an instrument for carrying out a Divine purpose"
- Historians have noted that his evangelical background shaped his sympathy for Zionist aims
